Daniel C. Howe is an artist, researcher and critical technologist whose work focuses on the social and political implications of networked and computational technologies, specifically concerning privacy, surveillance, and human rights. He has a Ph.D. in computer science and Masters in interactive media and digital literature. He divides his time between New York and Hong Kong, where he teaches at the School of Creative Media.
